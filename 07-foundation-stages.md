# Foundation Stages

These are the upstream design stages that should happen before detailed wireframes and page contracts.

They come from the original `idea-to-page-contract` workflow and belong in the portable kit because they prevent shallow or generic UI work.

## Stage A: Idea Capture

Goal:

- capture raw intent before structure hardens

Questions:

- what problem are we solving
- who is it for
- what should it feel like
- what should it not feel like

Gate:

- the problem can be described in one sentence

## Stage B: Mental Model

Goal:

- define what the user believes they are doing

Questions:

- what metaphor best fits the product
- what is the single most common thing the user does
- how would the user explain the product to a friend

Gate:

- a one-sentence user mental model exists

## Stage C: Information Architecture

Goal:

- define domains, entities, and boundaries before screen design becomes too literal

Questions:

- what are the major nouns
- which domain owns each noun
- what should be top-level vs nested

Gate:

- it is clear what lives where and why

## Why These Matter

Without these stages:

- screen inventories become feature piles
- navigation becomes flat and noisy
- prototypes become visually polished but structurally wrong
- page contracts map to bad product structure
